Safely Incorporating Actives into Your Skincare Routine

Navigating the world of skincare actives can be daunting. From chemical exfoliants to retinoids, the journey to incorporating these powerful ingredients into your routine requires patience and knowledge. This guide aims to simplify the process, offering practical steps and essential precautions to help you make the most of actives like AHAs, BHAs, azelaic acid, retinoids, and benzoyl peroxide.

Disclaimer: Before adding any actives to your routine, consult with a dermatologist. This guide is intended to assist with basic queries and provide a structured approach, not to replace professional advice.

Step 1: Sunscreen is a Must

Sunscreen is crucial when using actives. Most actives, especially AHAs and retinoids, increase sensitivity to the sun, making your skin more prone to damage. Even though BHAs and azelaic acid are less likely to cause photosensitivity, it’s best to use sunscreen whenever you’re incorporating actives into your routine.

Step 2: How to Introduce Actives

A) Patch Testing:

Before fully incorporating a new active, patch test to check for allergies and immediate reactions. For actives in cleansers or masks, irritation may become apparent sooner.

B) When and How Often to Use Actives:

  • AHAs: Use in the PM, once a day.
  • BHAs: Use in the AM or PM, once a day.
  • Azelaic Acid: Use in the AM or PM, once a day (up to twice a day if recommended by a dermatologist).
  • Retinoids: Use in the PM only, once a day.
  • Benzoyl Peroxide: Use in the PM, once a day (up to twice a day if recommended by a dermatologist).

C) Introducing Actives Slowly:

Start with a low frequency to gauge your skin’s reaction. Begin with once a week, then gradually increase to twice a week, and so on. Monitor for irritation and adjust accordingly. For sensitive skin, use smaller amounts and consider short contact therapy (SCT).

D) Application and Waiting Time:

Apply actives on a freshly cleansed, dry face. Wait at least 15 minutes before applying other products to ensure effectiveness, though the specific wait time can vary. If the active is causing dryness, you can apply hydrating products immediately.

E) Order of Application:

Apply actives after cleansing and before other treatments. Exceptions include products designed as masks or cleansers with actives.

F) Precautions:

  • Always use sunscreen.
  • Incorporate hydrating and moisturizing products to counteract dryness.
  • Start slowly to minimize irritation.

G) Combining Actives:

For beginners, it’s best to use one active at a time. If you want to combine actives, ensure they have compatible pH levels and allow adequate wait times between applications. You can also use actives as spot treatments or incorporate them into different parts of your routine.

H) Handling Negative Reactions:

If you experience irritation or damage, stop using all actives and focus on soothing and hydrating your skin. Use products with calming ingredients like aloe vera or hyaluronic acid.

Conclusion

Incorporating actives into your skincare routine requires a thoughtful approach. Start slowly, listen to your skin, and adjust as needed. With patience and the right techniques, you can reap the benefits of these potent ingredients without compromising your skin’s health.

FAQs

Is this guide applicable to Indian formulations?
Yes, this guide applies to various formulations, including those available in India.

Can I use an AHA/BHA cleanser followed by another AHA/BHA product?
Yes, but ensure your skin can tolerate it and that the total acid percentage doesn’t exceed recommended levels.

What if my dermatologist’s advice conflicts with this guide?
Follow your dermatologist's instructions. This guide is a general reference and may need to be adjusted based on your specific needs.

Can I use this guide with products from brands like The Ordinary?
Absolutely.

How do I combine actives effectively?
Follow the AM/PM schedule outlined and adjust based on pH levels and skin tolerance.
