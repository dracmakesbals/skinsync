Cleansing Is The Key to a Healthy Skincare Routine

Step 1: Cleanse – The Foundation of Your Skincare Routine

Welcome back! In my previous post, we dived into how to identify your skin type and some common skin conditions. Today, we’re going to take the first step in building a solid skincare routine – cleansing. It’s one of the most important steps for maintaining healthy skin, but it’s also one that can be confusing for beginners. Let's simplify it!

Why Cleansing Matters

The skin on your face is more sensitive than the rest of your body and constantly exposed to dirt, oils, and pollutants. Cleansing helps remove these impurities, prevents clogged pores, and preps your skin for the next steps in your routine.

Types of Cleansers

Water Alone – Using just water can help remove hydrophilic dirt, but it’s not enough to tackle oils and makeup. If you apply a rich cream or oil before bed, a cleanser in the morning is a must!

Micellar Water – A popular and gentle cleanser made with micelles (tiny balls of cleansing oil molecules) suspended in water. It’s great for removing makeup and grime without irritation. Pro tip: Always rinse with water afterward for best results.

Some great options: Bioderma Sensibio, Simple Kind to Skin Micellar Water

Regular Cleansers – Most cleansers use surfactants to break down oil and dirt. However, harsh surfactants and high pH levels can strip your skin’s natural barrier, leading to irritation. Look for gentle cleansers with mild surfactants and a skin-friendly pH.

Gentle options: Glossier Milky Jelly, The Ordinary Squalane Cleanser, Hada Labo Goku-jyun Foaming Cleanser

Double Cleansing – The Game-Changer

If you’re dealing with excess oil, makeup, or sunscreen, oil cleansing is your new best friend. Double cleansing involves starting with an oil-based cleanser to break down stubborn oils and dirt, followed by a regular cleanser. It’s perfect for all skin types, not just dry skin!

Why oil cleansing works: It draws out sebum, unclogs pores, and reduces irritation from harsh cleansers. You can use 100% cold-pressed oils or opt for a pre-made cleansing oil.

Cleansing oils available: L'Occitane Almond Shower Oil, Innisfree Camellia Nourishing Shower Oil

The Lazy Hack – Cleansing Oils/Balms

If double cleansing sounds like too much work, cleansing oils or balms might be your go-to. These combine both steps – massage onto the skin, add water to emulsify, and rinse. Voilà, clean skin in half the time!

Final Tips for Cleanser Shopping

Check Ingredients – Use sites like COSDNA and InciDecoder to decode ingredients and avoid irritants.

Start Gentle – Always test new cleansers to ensure they don’t disrupt your skin’s barrier.
